Hi! Welcome to my personal website!

I am Hannah Gonzalez (she/her), a candidate for a Master's of Science in Engineering and a Bachelor's of Science in Engineering in Computer Science at the University of Pennsylvania. I am pursuing minors in Mathematics and Engineering Entrepreneurship as well.

My research interests include natural language processing and machine learning. Foundation models fascinate me and I want to contribute towards their better understanding.

I am currently a Research Assistant in Professor Chris Callison-Burch's lab in the smart-textbook group. We study the applicability of question generation models to textbook passages. Check out our 2022 ACL paper "A Feasibility Study of Answer-Agnostic Question Generation for Education"!

Additionally, as a Research Assistant to Dr. Kathryn Davis at Penn's Center for Neuroengineering and Therapeutics (CNT), I trained a deep learning model to automate resection cavity segmentation on postoperative MRI of epilepsy patients to help physicians quantify removed brain structures. Take a look at our NeuroImage Clinical paper!

Mentoring is really important to me. If you are an undergraduate student interested in doing NLP research, please feel free to reach out to me! I am always happy to talk about research or careers in CS.

    Projects
    • Study Buddy
      • ×
      Title: Study Buddy
      Team: Hannah Gonzalez and Ahmed Ahmed
      Duration: August 2022 - May 2023
      [poster]

      When you're a student preparing for an exam, what's your study strategy? Do you anticipate the questions your professor might ask or do you enlist a friend to quiz you? With Study Buddy, studying for your classes becomes simple and efficient! Study Buddy is an Alexa app that tests your knowledge of lecture material. Our team has developed cutting-edge technology that generates relevant questions from a lecture video. Once generated, students can ask Alexa to quiz them on a particular lecture, making studying a breeze.
